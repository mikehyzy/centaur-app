Brand Guide v1

Centaur

An instrument for judgment. Sage/Magician archetype. Competence and Sophistication dominant. Bloomberg terminal restraint, consumer product legibility.

Color

Cool, restrained. Two-color floor — near-black background and cool off-white — with a single teal accent that reads as a measurement indicator. No warm tones. No gradients.

The three classification colors share identical perceptual lightness (L=0.70) and chroma (C=0.065). Only hue varies. This ensures no color implies a valence — Direct is not "good," Defer is not "bad."

Typography

Two typefaces. IBM Plex Sans for all interface text — data, labels, navigation, body. Instrument Serif for moments of authority: the classification verdict, the drift indicator, section headings.

Classifications

Direct, Delegate, and Defer are the product's primary visual vocabulary. Typographic differentiation is the primary signal — weight, case, and family. Color is secondary, with three perceptually equal, non-valenced hues.
